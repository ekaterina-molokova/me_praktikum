4 rules of markup

Emptiness

A simple rule that is often broken: put elements with a similar meaning next to each other and surround them with empty space. Emptiness is a powerful means of influence.

Grid

The dimensions of the elements and the distances between them fit into a harmonious pattern - it is called a grid. If an element falls out, the viewer can notice it.

Fonts

No more than three fonts per site. One for headings, the second for all other texts, the third for highlighting important words. Should be enough.

Colors

Usually, a website has two colors: leading and accent. You can choose a successful color pair through observation or special tools.
